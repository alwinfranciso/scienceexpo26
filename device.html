Device Management

How the computer talks to hardware like printers and mice.

Communication Layers

The Operating System acts as a bridge. It translates your simple clicks and typing into signals that the physical hardware can understand.

Core Concepts

Device Drivers

Software that acts as a translator between the Operating System and the specific hardware device.

Interrupts

A signal from a device to the CPU saying "I'm done" or "I need attention right now."

Buffering

A temporary "waiting room" for data being moved between a fast CPU and a slower device.

Spooling

Placing jobs (like printing) in a queue so they can be processed one by one in the background.

Transfer Techniques

Method Simple Explanation Efficiency
Polling The CPU keeps asking the device "Are you ready yet?" Low (Wastes CPU time)
Interrupt-Driven The device calls the CPU only when it's actually ready. Medium (Good for slow tasks)
DMA Direct Memory Access. The device talks to RAM directly. High (Fastest method)
